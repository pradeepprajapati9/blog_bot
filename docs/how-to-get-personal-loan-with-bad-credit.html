How to Get Personal Loan with Bad Credit

30 Jun 2026

Getting a personal loan can be challenging when you have a low credit score, often referred to as 'bad credit'. Many traditional lenders prefer applicants with a strong credit history, making it seem like a closed door. However, it's not always impossible to secure a personal loan even if your credit score isn't ideal. This guide will walk you through understanding your situation, exploring viable options in India, and adopting smart strategies to improve your chances of approval, even with a less-than-perfect credit record. It's about knowing where to look and what steps to take.

Understanding Your Credit Score and Why It Matters

Your credit score, primarily your CIBIL score in India, is a three-digit number between 300 and 900 that reflects your creditworthiness. A score below 650-700 is generally considered 'bad' or 'low credit'. Lenders use this score to assess the risk of lending money to you. A low score signals a higher risk of default, making banks hesitant to approve unsecured personal loans. Factors contributing to a bad score include missed EMI payments, high credit utilization, multiple loan rejections, and too many credit inquiries. Before seeking a loan, it's crucial to know your exact score and understand the reasons behind it. This knowledge empowers you to explain your situation to potential lenders and work towards improvement.

First Step: Check Your Credit Report and Rectify Errors

The very first practical step is to obtain a copy of your credit report from CIBIL or other credit bureaus like Experian, Equifax, and Crif Highmark. You are entitled to one free credit report annually from each bureau. Carefully review the report for any inaccuracies or errors. Mistakes can include incorrect personal details, loans you never took, or accounts that are shown as active but you’ve closed. If you find any discrepancies, immediately raise a dispute with the respective credit bureau. Correcting errors can sometimes significantly improve your score overnight, making you a more attractive borrower. Don't skip this crucial verification step; it's your financial fingerprint.

Exploring Lenders Who May Consider Bad Credit Applicants

While traditional banks might be strict, certain lenders are more open to applicants with bad credit, often at higher interest rates. These include Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), which sometimes have more flexible eligibility criteria than banks. Peer-to-Peer (P2P) lending platforms also connect borrowers directly with individual lenders who might be willing to take a higher risk for better returns. Some new-age fintech lenders focus on alternative data points beyond just your credit score, such as your income stability, employment history, and banking habits. Research these options carefully, compare their terms, and always verify their legitimacy to avoid fraudulent schemes. Be prepared for potentially higher processing fees and stricter repayment schedules.

Secured Loan Options and Co-applicant Strategies

If an unsecured personal loan is out of reach, consider secured loan options. These loans require collateral, which reduces the risk for the lender. Common secured loans include a Gold Loan, where your gold jewellery acts as security, or a Loan Against Property (LAP), using your real estate. You might also consider a Loan Against Securities if you have investments like shares or mutual funds. Another effective strategy is to apply for a personal loan with a co-applicant or guarantor who has a good credit score. This person's strong credit profile can significantly bolster your application, making it more appealing to lenders. Ensure both parties understand the responsibilities, as the co-applicant becomes equally liable for the loan.

How to Improve Your Credit Score for Future Needs

While seeking a loan with bad credit, simultaneously work on improving your credit score for better financial health in the long run. The most impactful actions include paying all your EMIs and credit card bills on time, every time. Even a single missed payment can negatively impact your score. Secondly, reduce your credit utilization ratio by paying down existing debt; aim to keep it below 30% of your total credit limit. Avoid applying for multiple loans or credit cards simultaneously, as each inquiry can temporarily lower your score. Regularly monitor your credit report for any changes or errors. Over time, consistent good financial habits will reflect positively on your score, making future loans easier and cheaper to obtain.

Beware of Predatory Lenders and High-Interest Traps

When you're in urgent need of a loan and have bad credit, it's easy to fall prey to unscrupulous lenders offering 'guaranteed approvals' with no credit checks. Exercise extreme caution. These lenders often charge exorbitantly high interest rates, hidden fees, and employ aggressive recovery tactics. Always verify the lender's registration and legitimacy with regulatory bodies like the RBI. Read the loan agreement carefully, understanding all terms, conditions, and charges before signing anything. Never pay an upfront fee for a loan approval – this is a common red flag for scams. A legitimate lender will always conduct due diligence, even if they specialize in bad credit loans, and will be transparent about their terms.

Conclusion

Getting a personal loan with bad credit in India is challenging, but not an impossible feat. It requires diligence, careful research, and a willingness to explore alternative avenues. By understanding your credit situation, correcting errors, exploring specific lenders, considering secured options, and perhaps applying with a co-applicant, you can increase your chances. Crucially, while addressing your immediate need, commit to improving your credit score for a more stable financial future. Always choose legitimate lenders and understand all terms to protect yourself from predatory practices.
