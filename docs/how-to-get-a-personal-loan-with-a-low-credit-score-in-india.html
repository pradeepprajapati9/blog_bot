How to Get a Personal Loan with a Low Credit Score in India

28 Jun 2026

Getting a personal loan in India can be challenging, especially if your credit score is less than ideal. Many lenders view a low credit score as a red flag, indicating a higher risk of default. However, having a low credit score doesn't mean all your options are closed. This guide will walk you through practical strategies and alternative avenues to explore when you need a personal loan but your credit score is holding you back. We'll focus on actionable steps tailored for Indian borrowers, helping you understand how to improve your chances and secure the funds you need.

Understanding Why Lenders Care About Your Credit Score

Before diving into solutions, it's crucial to understand why your credit score matters so much to lenders in India. Your credit score, primarily your CIBIL score (or Experian, Equifax, CRIF High Mark), is a three-digit number between 300 and 900 that summarizes your creditworthiness. A score of 750 or above is generally considered good. Lenders use this score to assess the likelihood of you repaying your loan on time. A low score (typically below 650-700) suggests a history of missed payments, defaults, or high credit utilization, making lenders hesitant to approve your application. They see it as a higher risk, which is why traditional personal loans become difficult to obtain. Understanding this helps you appreciate why you need to approach the situation strategically and consider alternatives or ways to mitigate their perceived risk.

Check and Understand Your Credit Report

The first step is to know exactly where you stand. Obtain a copy of your full credit report from CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, or CRIF High Mark. You are entitled to one free credit report annually from each bureau. Review it thoroughly for any errors or inaccuracies. Sometimes, a low score is due to incorrect reporting, such as payments you made being marked as missed, or accounts you don't recognize. If you find errors, dispute them immediately with the credit bureau. Correcting these errors can quickly improve your score. Also, understand the reasons for your low score – whether it's delayed payments, high credit utilization, or too many recent credit inquiries. This self-awareness is key to addressing the root cause and demonstrating to lenders that you are aware of your financial standing and actively working to improve it.

Explore Lenders Specializing in Low Credit Score Loans

While traditional banks might be strict, some Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and digital lenders in India are more flexible. These lenders often have different eligibility criteria and may be willing to lend to individuals with lower credit scores, albeit often at higher interest rates. They might look at other factors like your current income stability, employment history, or assets. Research specific NBFCs known for offering loans to borrowers with sub-par credit scores. Be prepared for a more thorough scrutiny of your current financial situation and employment stability. Always compare interest rates, processing fees, and other terms from multiple lenders to ensure you're getting the best possible deal under the circumstances.

Apply with a Co-Applicant or Guarantor

One of the most effective ways to secure a personal loan with a low credit score is to apply with a co-applicant or guarantor who has a good credit score. When you apply jointly, the lender considers the creditworthiness of both applicants. If your co-applicant has a strong credit history and stable income, it significantly reduces the perceived risk for the lender. A guarantor also acts as a backup, promising to repay the loan if you default. This added security makes lenders more comfortable approving your application, even with your low score. Ensure your co-applicant or guarantor fully understands their responsibilities, as their credit score will also be affected if payments are missed.

Consider Secured Personal Loans

If unsecured personal loans are proving difficult, consider a secured personal loan. Unlike traditional personal loans which require no collateral, a secured loan requires you to pledge an asset as security. This could be gold, property, fixed deposits, or even shares. By offering collateral, you significantly reduce the lender's risk, making them more likely to approve your loan despite a low credit score. Lenders often offer better interest rates on secured loans because of the reduced risk. Examples include gold loans, loans against property, or loans against fixed deposits. While it carries the risk of losing your asset if you default, it can be a viable option to get urgent funds when your credit score is a major hurdle.

Focus on Improving Your Credit Score

While you're exploring immediate loan options, concurrently work on improving your credit score. This is a long-term solution that will benefit you immensely in the future. Start by making all your existing EMI and credit card payments on time, every time. Reduce your credit utilization ratio by paying off outstanding credit card balances; aim to keep it below 30%. Avoid applying for too many new credit products simultaneously, as each inquiry can temporarily lower your score. Consider taking a small secured loan or a credit-builder loan and repaying it diligently. Over time, consistent good financial behavior will gradually build a stronger credit profile, making it easier to access credit at favorable terms in the future. This proactive approach is crucial for long-term financial health.

Micro-Loans and Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Lending Platforms

For smaller loan amounts, consider micro-lenders or Peer-to-Peer (P2P) lending platforms. Micro-lenders often cater to individuals with limited credit history or lower scores, focusing more on current income stability and repayment capacity. P2P platforms connect borrowers directly with individual investors. While interest rates can sometimes be higher on P2P platforms, they often have more flexible eligibility criteria compared to traditional banks. Investors on these platforms may be willing to take a higher risk based on your overall profile, income, and loan purpose, rather than solely relying on a high credit score. Always verify the legitimacy of the platform and compare terms carefully before committing to a loan through these channels. Read reviews and understand all associated fees.

Conclusion

Getting a personal loan with a low credit score in India requires a strategic approach. While it presents challenges, it is certainly not impossible. By understanding your credit report, exploring alternative lenders, applying with a co-applicant, considering secured options, or utilizing P2P platforms, you can increase your chances of securing the necessary funds. Remember, simultaneously focusing on improving your credit score through diligent financial practices will pave the way for easier and more affordable credit access in the long run. Always borrow responsibly and ensure you can comfortably repay any loan you take.
