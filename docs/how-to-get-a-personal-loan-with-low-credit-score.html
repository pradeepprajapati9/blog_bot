How to Get a Personal Loan with Low Credit Score

27 Jun 2026

Getting a personal loan in India can be challenging if you have a low credit score. Lenders view a low score, typically below 650-700 for CIBIL, as a sign of higher risk, making them hesitant to approve your application. However, a low score doesn't necessarily mean all doors are closed. While it requires a more strategic approach, there are still ways to explore options for securing a personal loan. This guide will walk you through practical steps and considerations to help you navigate the process, understand your alternatives, and improve your financial standing for future borrowing.

Understand Your Credit Score's Impact and Your Eligibility

Before applying, it's crucial to know your exact credit score and understand what constitutes a 'low' score in India. Generally, a CIBIL score below 650-700 is considered low by most mainstream banks. Lenders use this score to assess your creditworthiness and repayment history. A low score indicates potential past payment defaults, high credit utilisation, or a short credit history, increasing the perceived risk for the lender. Understanding why your score is low (e.g., missed EMI payments, too many credit card applications) can help you address those issues. Obtain your credit report from agencies like CIBIL, Experian, or Equifax to check for errors and get a clear picture of your financial standing.

Explore Non-Traditional Lenders and NBFCs

While major banks might be stringent, several Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and new-age fintech lenders in India are more willing to consider applicants with lower credit scores. These lenders often have more flexible eligibility criteria and assess applications based on a broader set of factors beyond just the credit score, such as your current income stability, employment history, and debt-to-income ratio. Research and identify reputable NBFCs and digital lending platforms that specifically market to individuals with less-than-perfect credit. Be prepared for potentially higher interest rates and stricter repayment terms compared to traditional bank loans, as the lender is taking on a greater risk.

Consider Secured Personal Loans or Gold Loans

One effective way to secure a loan with a low credit score is to offer collateral. A secured personal loan reduces the risk for the lender, making them more likely to approve your application. Common forms of collateral in India include gold, property, fixed deposits, or even shares. Gold loans, in particular, are very popular and accessible across India. You can pledge your gold ornaments or coins with a bank or NBFC and get a loan based on their value. These loans typically have lower interest rates than unsecured personal loans, even for individuals with a low credit score, due to the inherent security they offer.

Apply with a Co-Applicant or Guarantor

Another viable option is to apply for a personal loan with a co-applicant or a guarantor who has a strong credit score and stable income. When you apply with a co-applicant, both your incomes and credit profiles are considered, which can significantly boost your eligibility. Similarly, a guarantor with a good credit history provides an assurance to the lender that someone will repay the loan if you default. This greatly mitigates the lender's risk, increasing your chances of approval. Ensure your co-applicant or guarantor fully understands their responsibility before proceeding, as their credit score will also be impacted if payments are missed.

Demonstrate Stable Income and Repayment Capacity

Even with a low credit score, strong proof of income and repayment capacity can sometimes sway a lender. Prepare thorough documentation showing your current employment stability, consistent income, and any other assets you possess. This includes recent salary slips (for salaried individuals), bank statements for the last 6-12 months, Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the past few years, and proof of any other regular income sources. Lenders want to see that you have a consistent cash flow that can comfortably cover the loan's EMIs, indicating your ability to repay despite past credit challenges. A high debt-to-income ratio might still be a hurdle, so ensure your existing debt burden is manageable.

Opt for Smaller Loan Amounts and Shorter Tenures

When your credit score is low, lenders are less likely to sanction large loan amounts. Applying for a smaller personal loan amount significantly increases your chances of approval. A smaller loan means lower EMIs, which makes it easier for you to repay and reduces the risk for the lender. Similarly, opting for a shorter repayment tenure can also be beneficial. While shorter tenures mean higher EMIs, they reduce the overall interest paid and signal to the lender that you are committed to clearing the debt quickly, reflecting a responsible borrowing approach. Successfully repaying a smaller loan can also help improve your credit score for future, larger borrowing needs.

Steps to Improve Your Credit Score for Future Loans

While seeking a loan with a low score, it's equally important to work on improving your credit health for better future opportunities. Start by consistently paying all your EMIs and credit card bills on time. Aim to keep your credit utilisation ratio below 30% – meaning, if your credit card limit is ₹1,00,000, try not to spend more than ₹30,000. Avoid applying for multiple loans or credit cards simultaneously, as each application can temporarily lower your score. Regularly check your credit report for errors and dispute any inaccuracies promptly. Over time, diligent financial habits will lead to a healthier credit score, opening doors to more favourable loan terms and lower interest rates.

Conclusion

Obtaining a personal loan with a low credit score in India is undoubtedly more challenging, but it is not impossible. By understanding your credit situation, exploring alternative lenders, considering secured loan options, leveraging co-applicants, and demonstrating strong repayment capacity, you can increase your chances of approval. Remember, securing such a loan might involve higher interest rates or stricter terms. Simultaneously, commit to improving your credit score through disciplined financial practices. This proactive approach will not only help you meet your immediate financial needs but also build a stronger foundation for a healthier financial future.
