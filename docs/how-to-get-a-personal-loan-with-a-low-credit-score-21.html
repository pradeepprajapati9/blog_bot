How to Get a Personal Loan with a Low Credit Score

28 Jun 2026

In India, your CIBIL score acts as your financial report card. Most traditional banks hesitate to approve personal loans if your credit score falls below 650. However, an unexpected medical emergency, urgent home repair, or critical family expense does not wait for your credit score to improve. While securing a loan with a low credit score is challenging, it is certainly not impossible. By understanding how the Indian lending ecosystem works and exploring alternative financial pathways, you can successfully secure the funds you need. This practical guide covers legitimate and effective methods to obtain a personal loan even if your credit history is less than perfect.

Understand Your Score and Check for Errors

Before applying for any loan, you must know exactly where you stand. In India, credit scores range from 300 to 900, with anything below 650 generally considered low. Your first step should be to download your free annual credit report from CIBIL, Experian, or Equifax. Carefully examine the report for any errors. Sometimes, a loan you fully paid off might still show as active, or a spelling mistake in your name might have linked someone else's defaults to your profile. If you find any discrepancies, raise a dispute immediately on the credit bureau's official website. Correcting these simple administrative errors can instantly boost your score, making you a much more attractive candidate for traditional lenders without any extra financial effort.

Apply Jointly with a Co-Applicant or Guarantor

If your credit score is poor, one of the most reliable ways to secure a personal loan is by adding a co-applicant or a guarantor to your application. This person should ideally be an immediate family member, such as a spouse, parent, or sibling, who possesses a strong credit score of 750 or above and a stable income source. When you apply jointly, the lender assesses the creditworthiness of both individuals. Because the co-applicant becomes equally responsible for repaying the debt, the lender's risk drops significantly. This arrangement not only increases your chances of fast approval but can also help you negotiate a much more reasonable interest rate, saving you money over the tenure of the loan.

Provide Strong Proof of Your Income and Employment

Lenders ultimately want to know if you have the financial capacity to repay what you borrow. If your low credit score was caused by past financial mistakes that are now resolved, you can offset this by showcasing your current income stability. If you work for a highly reputed multinational company, a public sector undertaking, or an established government institution, highlight this in your application. Gather your latest salary slips, Form 16, income tax returns for the past two years, and bank statements showing consistent monthly salary credits. Showing a steady, reliable cash flow proves to the lender that your present financial situation is secure, making them more willing to overlook a historical low credit score.

Approach Non-Banking Financial Companies and Fintech Lenders

Traditional public and private sector banks have strict, automated credit score cut-offs. If your score is low, your application there might be rejected instantly. Instead, target Non-Banking Financial Companies and modern digital fintech lenders. NBFCs and mobile lending apps often have more flexible lending criteria. They utilize advanced algorithms to look beyond just your CIBIL score, assessing alternative data points such as your monthly utility bill payments, transaction history, and overall digital footprint. While these institutions are more likely to approve your personal loan, keep in mind that they often charge higher interest rates to compensate for the higher risk of lending to individuals with poor credit.

Consider Secured Loans as an Alternative

If unsecured personal loans remain out of reach, a secured loan is an excellent alternative. Secured loans require you to pledge an asset as collateral, virtually eliminating the lender's risk. In India, gold loans are highly popular and incredibly fast to process, requiring minimal documentation and zero credit score checks. Alternatively, you can take a loan against your fixed deposits, insurance policies, or mutual fund holdings. Banks are usually happy to lend up to 80 to 90 percent of the value of these assets. Because the loan is backed by collateral, the interest rates are typically much lower than standard personal loans, and your low credit score will not be a barrier to approval.

Request a Lower Loan Amount to Reduce Risk

When you have a low credit score, asking for a massive loan amount sends a red flag to lenders, signalling high risk and potential financial distress. To improve your approval odds, keep your loan request modest. Calculate the absolute minimum amount you need to manage your emergency and apply only for that sum. A smaller loan amount means smaller monthly installments, which lenders feel more confident you can repay from your current salary. Successfully securing and repaying a small personal loan on time is also one of the fastest ways to rebuild your credit history, paving the way for easier loan approvals and better interest rates in the future.

Conclusion

Securing a personal loan with a low credit score in India requires strategy, patience, and realistic expectations. By fixing report errors, applying with a co-signer, showcasing a stable income, or opting for secured collateral like gold, you can navigate financial emergencies successfully. Once you secure your loan, ensure you pay every single EMI on time to steadily rebuild your credit score for a secure financial future.
