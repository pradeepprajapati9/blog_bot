How to Get a Personal Loan with a Low Credit Score

28 Jun 2026

Getting a personal loan in India can feel like a challenge, especially when your credit score isn't ideal. A low credit score often signals to lenders that you might be a higher risk, making them hesitant to approve your application. However, a less-than-perfect credit score doesn't mean all doors are closed. Many Indians face this situation, and there are practical steps and alternative avenues you can explore. This guide will help you understand why your score matters, how to potentially improve it, and what options are available to secure the funds you need, even with a low credit standing.

Understanding Your Low Credit Score in India

Your credit score, primarily your CIBIL score in India, is a three-digit number between 300 and 900 that represents your creditworthiness. A score below 650-700 is generally considered low by most lenders. This score is calculated based on your past repayment history, credit utilization, types of credit accounts, and credit inquiry patterns. Lenders use it to assess the risk of lending to you. A low score might be due to missed EMI payments, high credit card balances, too many loan applications in a short period, or even having no credit history at all. Understanding the specific reasons behind your low score is the first step towards addressing it and improving your chances of loan approval.

Strategies to Improve Your Credit Score First

Before applying for a loan, focusing on improving your credit score can significantly enhance your chances. Start by obtaining your credit report from CIBIL, Experian, or Equifax to check for errors and understand the issues. Dispute any inaccuracies immediately. Next, prioritize clearing any outstanding dues and making all your EMI and credit card payments on time, every time. Timely payments are the biggest determinant of your score. Try to reduce your credit utilization ratio by paying down credit card balances; ideally, keep it below 30% of your total credit limit. Avoid applying for multiple loans or credit cards simultaneously, as each inquiry can temporarily dip your score. Building a good payment history consistently over several months will gradually improve your score.

Exploring Lenders More Open to Lower Scores

While traditional banks might be strict, certain lenders are more flexible. Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) often have slightly relaxed credit score requirements compared to major banks. Many newer FinTech lenders also offer personal loans with innovative assessment models that consider factors beyond just your CIBIL score, such as your income stability, employment history, and digital footprint. Additionally, some cooperative banks or regional rural banks might have different eligibility criteria. It's crucial to research these lenders thoroughly, compare their interest rates, processing fees, and terms, as they might be higher for individuals with low credit scores. Always verify their legitimacy and read reviews before applying.

Alternative Loan Options for Low Credit Scores

If a conventional personal loan remains out of reach, consider secured loan options. These loans require you to pledge an asset as collateral, reducing the risk for the lender and often making them more accessible even with a low credit score. Popular options in India include a gold loan (against your gold jewellery), a loan against property, or a loan against fixed deposits or mutual funds. The interest rates for secured loans are typically lower than unsecured personal loans because of the collateral. Another option could be a small personal loan from certain FinTech platforms specializing in micro-loans, which might have less stringent credit requirements for smaller amounts. Always ensure you can comfortably repay any secured loan to avoid losing your pledged asset.

Applying Smartly with a Less-Than-Perfect Score

When applying for a personal loan with a low credit score, strategy is key. Firstly, consider applying with a co-applicant or guarantor who has a strong credit score. Their good credit history can significantly boost your application's chances. Secondly, apply for a smaller loan amount than you initially considered, as lenders are more likely to approve smaller, less risky amounts. Be completely transparent about your financial situation and provide all necessary documents promptly and accurately. Showcase your current stable income and employment if possible. Prepare to explain the reasons for your low score and demonstrate how your financial situation has improved or will improve. Lastly, avoid applying to too many lenders at once, as multiple hard inquiries can further damage your credit score.

Avoiding Predatory Lenders and High-Cost Traps

When you're in need of funds and have a low credit score, it's easy to fall prey to predatory lenders. Be extremely cautious of lenders offering 'guaranteed approval' loans without any credit check, especially those demanding upfront fees before disbursing the loan. These are often scams. Always check if the lender is registered with the RBI (Reserve Bank of India) or is a legitimate NBFC. Unregistered lenders may charge exorbitant interest rates, have hidden charges, and employ aggressive recovery tactics. Carefully read the loan agreement, including the fine print, to understand all terms, conditions, interest rates, and penalties. If an offer seems too good to be true, it likely is. Prioritize your long-term financial health over quick, risky solutions.

Conclusion

While getting a personal loan with a low credit score in India presents challenges, it is not an impossible task. By understanding your credit situation, actively working to improve your score, exploring alternative lenders and secured loan options, and applying strategically, you can increase your chances of securing the necessary funds. Remember to always borrow responsibly, prioritize timely repayments, and avoid any dubious financial offers. Building and maintaining a good credit score is a continuous journey that will open up more financial opportunities for you in the long run.
