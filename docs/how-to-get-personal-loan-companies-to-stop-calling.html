How to Get Personal Loan Companies to Stop Calling

27 Jun 2026

Unsolicited calls from personal loan companies can be incredibly annoying and disruptive. For many Indian consumers, the constant ringing and persuasive pitches become a daily nuisance. You might wonder how these companies even get your number and, more importantly, how to make them stop. This guide provides practical, actionable steps to reclaim your peace and privacy from relentless telemarketing calls for personal loans.

Register for the DND (Do Not Disturb) Registry

The National Customer Preference Register (NCPR), previously known as the National Do Not Disturb (NDNC) registry, is your primary tool against unwanted calls and messages in India. By registering, you inform telemarketers that you do not wish to receive promotional communications. You can choose full blocking, which stops all commercial calls and SMS, or partial blocking, which allows certain categories like banking or health. To register, simply SMS 'START 0' to 1909 from your mobile number. Alternatively, you can call 1909 and follow the IVR instructions. It typically takes up to 7 days for your DND request to be fully activated across all telemarketers. Remember to recheck your status periodically, as some services might re-opt you in without explicit consent during plan changes or renewals.

Block Numbers Directly on Your Phone

While DND is a broader solution, immediate relief from specific persistent callers can come from blocking their numbers directly on your smartphone. Most Android and iOS devices offer built-in features to block unwanted numbers. When you receive a call from an unknown or unwanted number, simply open your call log, tap on the number, and look for an option like 'Block number,' 'Report Spam,' or 'Add to blacklist.' Blocking a number ensures that calls and messages from that specific contact will no longer reach you. This is a quick fix for individual nuisance callers, but be prepared to block multiple numbers as telemarketing companies often use various lines to circumvent blocks.

Explicitly Revoke Consent with the Caller

Often, personal loan companies have your number because you inadvertently provided consent somewhere – perhaps by filling an online form, using a financial app, or checking a box on a website. When a loan company calls, directly and politely inform them that you do not wish to receive further calls. State clearly that you are revoking any prior consent given for marketing communications and ask them to remove your number from their calling list immediately. It's helpful to note the company's name and, if possible, the name of the representative you spoke with. Follow up if calls continue from the same company. Under data protection principles, you have the right to withdraw your consent for marketing.

Report Persistent Violations to TRAI

If you have registered for DND and still receive unsolicited calls or SMS from personal loan companies after the 7-day activation period, you can report these violations to the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI). This is an important step to ensure compliance by telemarketers. To file a complaint, you can send an SMS with the complaint details (unsolicited commercial communication, date and time of call/SMS, sender's number) to 1909. Alternatively, you can use your telecom operator's app or website, which usually has a dedicated section for DND complaints. Provide accurate details to help TRAI investigate and take action against violating telemarketers. Consistent reporting helps enforce DND regulations effectively.

Be Cautious About Sharing Your Contact Information Online

Prevention is often better than cure. Many personal loan companies obtain your contact details from lead generation websites, third-party data brokers, or forms you fill out online for various services. Before submitting your phone number or email on any website or app, especially those offering comparisons or information on financial products, carefully read their privacy policy and terms of service. Look for checkboxes that ask for consent to share your data with partners or for marketing purposes. Untick these boxes if you do not wish to receive promotional calls. Being selective about where and how you share your personal information can significantly reduce the number of unsolicited calls you receive in the first place.

Contact Loan Companies Directly for Data Removal

If you have previously applied for a loan or inquired with a specific company and are now receiving unwanted calls from them, you have the right to request they remove your data from their marketing databases. Many reputable financial institutions have a clear process for this. Visit their official website and look for their customer service contact details, privacy policy, or a 'contact us' section. You can then send an email or call their customer service line, explicitly requesting that your number be removed from all marketing lists. Keep a record of your request, including the date and time, for your own reference. This direct approach can be very effective for companies you have previously interacted with, ensuring they comply with your wishes.

Conclusion

Dealing with persistent personal loan calls can be frustrating, but you have several effective strategies at your disposal to stop them. From registering for the DND service and blocking individual numbers to directly revoking consent and reporting persistent violators to TRAI, taking proactive steps can significantly reduce these unwanted interruptions. Remember to be mindful of where you share your contact information online to prevent future unsolicited calls. By implementing these measures, you can regain control over your phone and enjoy a much quieter, less bothered daily life.
